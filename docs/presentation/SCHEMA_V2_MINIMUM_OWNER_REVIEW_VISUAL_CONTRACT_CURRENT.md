The presentation helper emits deterministic, deep-copied descriptors shared by Gameplay drawing and focused validation. Each descriptor has a family, stable ID, cell/position, layer, state variant, shape/glyph/type-or-group token, scalar bounds and monochrome signature.

- Crates use box/cross structure.
- Keys and locks share stable sorted `K*` tokens; held keys use a bounded inventory strip.
- Sensors use structural `Y`, `E` and `A` glyphs and active/inactive rings.
- Barriers use stable `G*` tokens and closed, open or open-deferred structures.
- Latches use inactive diamond and persistent activated check structures, with their target group token.

The order is terrain, underlays/open edges, closed/interactable objects, actors, then inventory/tokens. These are minimum monochrome-safe owner-review semantics, not final art direction or accessibility acceptance.
