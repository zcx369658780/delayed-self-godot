## Accepted findings

### P2-1 — Difficulty curve is too flat and easy

The owner did not perceive a meaningful difficulty rise across the current five-level sequence. Machine validity and exact shortest-path evidence do not by themselves establish satisfying human difficulty. Further content multiplication is paused until the project defines stronger difficulty targets and evaluation proxies.

### P2-2 — Level 4 timing is mechanically valid but visually unclear

`door_one_turn_late` remains mechanically accepted: its shortest witness proves the closed-start/open-result/open-next-turn entry sequence. However, the owner found the transient change difficult to observe because the Door opens after one turn, is used on the next, and then can close again. This is a presentation/feedback deficiency, not a rejection of the underlying simulation rule.

### P2-3 — Timeline and `H` Help have little current practical value

The owner found neither the compact timeline nor expanded Help useful in the current simple puzzles. They are not removed or declared unnecessary, because harder levels, multi-delay content, and fresh-player evidence do not yet exist. Their continued presence must be reevaluated after difficulty recovery and presentation changes.

### P2-4 — Effective gameplay/board area feels too small

The application window may be larger, but the effective board region remains small relative to the HUD and unused presentation space. The issue is accepted as a layout/scale concern. This gate does not authorize a viewport, resolution, or scene-layout change.

### P3-1 — Separate latched/one-shot Gate proposal

The owner proposed a Gate that stays open or disappears after activation. This is not applied to the current combinational Plate Door. Current Door semantics remain frozen. A latched/one-shot Gate requires a separate architecture decision covering schema representation, state, canonical keys, restart, replay, solver behavior, migration, visual language, and level-design value.

### P3-2 — Licensed visual, audio, and narrative production direction

The owner requested replacement of primitive circles with coherent free/licensed assets, addition of music and sound effects, and a lightweight story. This is accepted as a production priority for planning, not as authority to download, import, or ship any particular asset.
